Topic 10: Code of ethical conduct for ksl interpretres

1 . Cofidentiality

Maintaining the privacy of all interpreted communication is paramount . interpereters must safeguard any sensitive or personal information shared during their services .

2 . Accuracy

interpreters must faithfully convey the message without adding omitting or altering meaning precision in deliverng the speaker's intent is essential .

3 . Impartiality

interpreters should remain neutral and unblased refraining from inserting personal opinions beliefs or influencing the community process in any way .

4 . Professional conduct

Maintaingand boundaries behavior and boundaries is expected at all times . this incudes punctuality appropriate attire and respectful interaction with all parties .

5 . Cultural sensitivity

Respect for the cultural norms and values of the deaf community in kenya is vital interpreters must be sensitive to cultural nuanas thataffect communication .

6 . Professional Development

Interpreters should continuously seek learning opportunities to maintain and improve their skills, knowledge, and ethical practice

7 . Conflict of Interest

Any potential or actual conflict of interest must be identified and managed. Interpreters must avoid situations that could compromise their impartiality or objectivity.

8 . Advocacy

While remaining neutral during interpretation, KSL interpreters have a responsibility to advocate for the rights and access needs of Deaf individuals, particularly by highlighting communication barriers or promoting better interpreting services when appropriate.

9 . Self-Awareness

Interpreters should recognize their own biases, limitations, and cultural influences. Awareness of these factors is critical for ethical and objective interpretation.

10 . Competence

Assignments should only be accepted when the interpreter possesses the necessary linguistic proficiency in KSL and relevant spoken languages, as well as subject matter knowledge. Accepting beyond one's capabilities may result in misinterpretation.

11 . Continuing Education

Interpreters have an ethical obligation to keep up with changes in language, technology, and best practices by engaging in ongoing education and training.

12 . Transparency

Open communication about any limitations or conflicts of interest with clients or relevant parties is essential. This enables informed decisions and appropriate arrangements.

13 . Respectful Communication

All interactions—whether with Deaf individuals, hearing clients, or other professionals—must be conducted with respect to ensure a positive and professional interpreting environment.

14 . Refusal of Assignments

Interpreters have both the right and responsibility to refuse assignments when they lack the required competence or face a conflict of interest, to protect the integrity of the interpretation.

15 . Professional Boundaries

Clear professional boundaries must be maintained at all times. Personal relationships with clients should be avoided to preserve professional integrity.

16 . Accessibility

Interpreters should work to ensure that interpreting services are accessible to all Deaf individuals, being mindful of differing needs and communication preferences.
